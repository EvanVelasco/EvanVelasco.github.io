Proration Script¶

To prorate this data, we will create a loop which divides each line item's revenue total by the number of days the deal is live. It is important to note that this method assumes even distribution of impressions across a deal's lifetime. Often this is not the case due to user traffic, inventory availability, or adjustments to the Deliver impressions setting in GAM. However, based on our domain knowledge of this data we know that nearly all line items are set with even distribution. Traffic fluctuations and inventory will still affect revenue distribution. However, for the purposes of analysis/forecasting this method will provide more than enough precision.

In [5]:
#create an empty list
appended_data = []

for i in np.arange(len(pg_df)):
    
    #define variables based on current row of data
    start = pg_df['start_date'][i]
    end = pg_df['end_date'][i]
    line_item_name = pg_df['name'][i]
    deal_id = pg_df['deal_id'][i]
    total_rev = pg_df['revenue'][i]

    #use the pandas date_range function to create a DatetimeIndex of values between the start and end date
    date_range = pd.date_range(start, end)
    
    #divide the total revenue for the deal by the number of days the deal is live
    daily_rev = total_rev / len(date_range)

    #create a temp dataframe based on the date range. add the calculated daily revenue and other columns to the data
    temp_df = date_range.to_frame(index=False, name='day')
    temp_df['revenue'] = daily_rev
    temp_df['line_item'] = line_item_name
    temp_df['deal_id'] = deal_id

    #append the temporary dataframe to the list
    appended_data.append(temp_df)

#concatenate all dataframes into a single dataframe outside of the list
final_df = pd.concat(appended_data)

#add additional date columns
final_df['month'] = final_df['day'].dt.month
final_df['year'] = final_df['day'].dt.year

While this is not a concern for such a small dataset, it is best practice and will allow this code to be scalable for larger datasets in the future.

Merging with Lookup Table for Additional Insights¶

We have additional information available about each deal in the line_item column. However, our business knowledge reminds us that the line_item column is not always 100% accurate. Even if we split using a _ delimiter the resulting data may require additional cleaning. Instead we will take advantage of a mapping file the company keeps for our PG deals. This mapping file is maintained by the financial incentive team so it will allow for some flexibility for things like a sales rep changing half way through the year.

The plot above shows us that January (Month 1) was our best month for PG revenue. There is also a concerning drop in revenue from August (Month 8) onward. Using our knowledge of advertising this drop actually makes sense since this data was pulled sometime mid June. Agencies/advertisers have likely not had time to start their Q3 or Q4 budgets. However, it is still an important trend to point out as it gives us an idea of where we currently stand. Should the later months of the year not see any improvement as we near the end of June, that would be a large concern and our forecasts to Finance should specifically point to this information.

Next Steps¶

The above insights are examples of the type of analysis this data allows for. Moving forward it is would be best to meet with sales leaders and determine what metrics are most useful to track. These views could be placed into an automated dashboard for continued monitoring and analysis.

In addition to analysis/dashboards, this data will be extremely important when providing revenue pacing information to Finance. It is a leading indicator for how future months will close. For example, July is likely to be a strong month for programmatic due already securing a large amount of PG revenue before the month has begun. June is likely to be a relatively average month looking at what is booked. It would require a large amount of in-month booking to become a strong PG month. Past experience has told us that large in-month bookings are typically rare (although not unheard of).
