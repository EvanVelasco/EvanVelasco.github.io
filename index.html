About Me

I began my career focused on programmatic data and understanding the complex landscape of ad tech. Since then, I have continued to gain knowledge of digital advertising as a whole. Education

I am a current student at Georgia Tech pursuing a Masters of Science in Analytics. My decision to go back to school was a natural progression as I continued to seek knowledge. I chose the program at Georgia Tech due to its academic rigour and wide array of course choices. I am most interested in learning about the mathematics behind modeling algorithms. A few courses I am looking forward to include Optimization, Regression, and High Dimensional Data Analytics.

I am a proud graduate of UC San Diego where I received a B.S. in Joint Mathematics-Economics. The Math-Econ major is very broad and allows for flexibility depending on an individual's interests. I chose to focus my coursework on statistics which introduced me to the field of analytics and data science. I had the pleasure of taking multiple computational statistics courses based in R and data science courses in Python. I paired my applied statistical courses with rigorous mathematical theory, including multiple courses in real analysis. My degree formed a strong foundation which I continue to build upon through self studying and professional experience.

Interests

Currently Studying:

Data Analytics for Business: This course covers methodologies, algorithms, and challenges related to analyzing business data. After many years of experience handling business data, much of the course content feels like a refresher. However, I am seizing this opportunity to explore foundational analytical concepts in greater depth. For example, I've taken the initiative to delve into the mathematical intricacies of the box cox transformation, extending beyond course materials. I have also spent time building algorithms from scratch to compute and showcase metadata for common analytical methods such as the variance inflation factor (VIF).

Sampling Theory and Methods: A lighter courseload has given me the time to study material outside of the OMSA curriculum. I've been reviewing sampling methodologies using the textbook Sampling by Thompson along with Penn State University's online course notes. My renewed interest in this subject was driven by recent applications of sampling I have seen at work. I am particularly interested in getting a more formal education in sampling techniques outside of simple random, such as stratified and cluster samples.
