Interests

Currently Studying:

Computational Data Analysis (Machine Learning 1): This course is a graduate introduction to machine learning, covering classical methods in both supervised and unsupervised learning. This is known as one of the highlight courses of the program, and so far I have been extremely impressed. Algorithms taught in the class are presented in great detail, with derivations and proofs for all aspects. This is a fantastic course to study after spending 5+ years in higher education covering topics like mathematics, statistics, optimization, and computer science. Everything comes together in this class.

Market Segmentation Analysis: In my free time, I am also reading through the book "Market Segmentation Analysis - Understanding It, Doing It, and Making It Useful" by Sara Dolnicar, Bettina Grün, and Friedrich Leisch. This is a great topic, and something that I think deserves more attention. Market segmentation is often brought up when students learn their first clustering algorithm. However, the reality is that building useful segmentation strategies is a difficult task. Unsupervised learning can give the illusion of simplicity, because your algorithm will always return a result. This book discusses the segmentation process in detail, including algorithms and validation techniques. One of my favorite aspects of this book is that it focuses on audience segmentation when no “natural” clusters exist, which is the reality in most business cases. You can download this book from Springer HERE.
